About me


Welcome to my personal blog. My name is Yunzhe Wang, and you may also address me by the Cantonese-inspired phonetic name, Wenzy Wong. Both are acceptable and reflect my given name.

I am currently a third-year student in the combined Master's and Ph.D. programme at Zhang Lab, School of Basic Medical Sciences, Fudan University.

Before the programme, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Bioinformatics at College of Informatics, Huazhong Agricultural University, where I systematically learned this field and developed both data-driven and question-driven thinking. Beyond traditional undergraduate studies, to compensate for the lack of exposure to medical research at an agricultural university, I also participated in a visiting research programme, Biological and Biomedical Science Programme, at School of Life Sciences, The Chinese University of Hong Kong. The group I led there won the second price in the ending presentation of this programme.

During my Ph.D. programme, I have continued to enhance bioinformatics skills, aiming to understand more of their effective applications in cancer-related studies. My current research focuses on utilizing multi-omics data to explore unique microbial communities associated with human gastrointestinal tract cancers. I am also deeply interested in interdisciplinary fields, such as the relationship between neuroactive substances and cancer, the impact of metabolites from various sources on epigenetic reprogramming, etc.
